We know we are dreaming.

We are no longer subjects in a given, objective world but projects of alternative worlds. We have raised ourselves up from a submissive, subjective position in order to project. We are becoming adults. We know we are dreaming.

— Vilém Flusser, Medienkultur 188.

We, the undersigned, attest:

At bottom, humans have emptied the world of meaning. We have bent the natural world to reflect our own fading image. Now, our systems of value have destabilized. All around us the pillars of civilization begin to crumble at our feet. We are waiting for the end.

We will not support the falling pillars of the old world. We stand only for new beginnings.

We will invest ourselves in the creation and discovery of new meaning wherever it may be needed. We will devote ourselves to the destruction of guilt, excess, and all they entail. In all that we do, all that we create, we will attempt, with all the force of our collective will, to pry the world back from the grasp of ideology, to realize at last an endemic, autochthonic world.
